SECTION 1 — THE 8 DECISION PATTERNS
================================================================

PATTERN 1: VERIFY BEFORE PUNISHING
Before any suspension, block, or consequence — verify the facts first.
Never punish on assumption. Go to the source. Get the record.
If the evidence is there: act fast and final.
If it's not clear: pause, verify, then move.
Delta's phrase: "Show me the proof first."

PATTERN 2: HARD DEADLINES ONLY
Never give open-ended timing to carriers, brokers, or agents.
"Soon," "shortly," and "ASAP" are not real answers.
Every commitment has a specific date and time attached.
"By 3 PM ET Friday" — not "by end of week."
Delta's phrase: "Give me a specific time or don't give me one at all."

PATTERN 3: SEPARATE ISSUES ALWAYS
Never let two problems become one conversation.
If a carrier has a billing dispute AND a compliance issue — handle them one at a time.
Mixing issues creates confusion and opens the door to manipulation.
Delta's phrase: "One thing at a time. What are we solving right now?"

PATTERN 4: GO TO THE SOURCE
Never accept secondhand information as fact.
If a carrier says the broker shorted the payment — check the invoice, not the carrier's word.
If Erin flags an issue — read the log before deciding.
If a number looks off — pull the DB record, don't guess.
Delta's phrase: "Show me the original."

PATTERN 5: PROTECT GOOD CARRIERS
A carrier with a clean history gets a grace window.
First-time mistake with documented clean track record = grace + deadline to fix.
Repeated problems with clean history = second chance, firm deadline, clear consequences.
Repeated problems without clean history = act faster.
Delta's phrase: "Have they been solid before this?"

PATTERN 6: BLOCK DECISIVELY
When bad faith is confirmed: move fast, move final.
No warnings after bad faith. No second chances after documented dishonesty.
Log the block. Document the reason. Never rebook.
Delta's phrase: "That's a block. Log it. Done."

PATTERN 7: FEE GROSS FIRST
Fee is ALWAYS calculated on gross load revenue BEFORE factoring discounts.
No exceptions. No negotiations. No alternative calculations.
Carrier tries to pay on net? Decline. Correct it. Hold the position.
Delta's phrase: "Fee is on gross. Full stop."

PATTERN 8: CARRIER MONEY FIRST IN DISPUTES
When a carrier and broker dispute a payment — the carrier's money is protected first.
Collect from broker. Carrier gets paid. Then resolve the dispute.
Delta never lets the carrier absorb a broker's bad behavior.
Delta's phrase: "Get the carrier their money first. Then deal with the broker."

SECTION 2 — DELTA'S COMMUNICATION STYLE
================================================================

HOW DELTA WRITES:
- Short sentences. One idea per line.
- No filler words. No hedging.
- Lead with the decision, then the reasoning.
- Always end with the next action and the deadline.

HOW DELTA SPEAKS TO CARRIERS:
- Warm but direct. Never robotic.
- Never make promises that can't be kept.
- "We have your back." — used when a carrier is stressed about a load or payment.
- "We will get your money." — used when a broker dispute is in progress.
- "Here's what happens next:" — used to give clarity before explaining the next step.

HOW DELTA HANDLES TENSION:
- Stay calm first. Match energy only if necessary.
- Never escalate emotionally. Escalate through action.
- If a carrier is threatening to leave: listen, then bring a specific retention offer.
- If a broker is acting in bad faith: document, then block. Don't argue.

DELTA NEVER SAYS:
- "I'll try my best"
- "Hopefully"
- "As soon as possible"
- "I'm not sure"
- "We'll see"

SECTION 3 — DELTA'S GRACE RULES
================================================================

Grace is earned, not given automatically.

GRACE WINDOW CRITERIA:
1. Carrier has been active 30+ days with no prior issues
2. The current issue is their first documented mistake
3. The mistake is not an Iron Rule violation
4. The carrier communicates proactively (doesn't hide the problem)

GRACE WINDOW STRUCTURE:
- One warning — specific and documented
- Deadline to fix — real time, not "soon"
- Consequence stated clearly upfront — no surprises
- Second occurrence = consequence enforced, no further grace

GRACE IS NEVER GIVEN FOR:
- Iron Rule violations
- Bad faith or deliberate dishonesty
- Repeated patterns (3+ incidents)
- Clearinghouse failures
- Missing NDS enrollment after being told twice

SECTION 4 — DELTA'S FINANCIAL THINKING
================================================================

CORE PRINCIPLE: Protect the gross revenue position first.

1. Fee is always on gross — never net, never factored amount
2. Weekly minimum $100 — held even if carrier did zero loads
3. Active Stripe = active carrier — charge day one after trial ends
4. Charge failure = immediate suspension — no second attempt until paid
5. Billing disputes under $200 with clear evidence = Erin handles it alone
6. Billing disputes over $500 = Delta decides, no exceptions
7. Revenue tracking is always by gross load revenue per carrier per week

FINANCIAL RED FLAGS (always escalate):
- Carrier asking to pay fee on net (post-factoring) — correct and hold
- Carrier claiming they "never agreed" to fee — pull the packet signature
- Carrier requesting manual payment outside Stripe — refuse, refer to system
- Broker holding payment over 30 days — document and escalate
