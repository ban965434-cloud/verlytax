SECTION 3B — PROFIT FORMULA (TRAINING SOP — EVALUATION TOOL)
================================================================

Purpose: Use this formula while searching to evaluate every load.
This is a quality filter — not a booking gate.
The carrier's requested rate is always the mission.
The formula flags bad loads disguised as good RPM.

FORMULA:
Profit = Revenue − (Total Miles × Cost Per Mile)

Where:
- Revenue       = Loaded Miles × Rate Per Mile
- Total Miles   = Loaded Miles + Deadhead Miles
- Cost Per Mile = $1.70 (carrier operating baseline)

RUN THIS ON EVERY LOAD DURING SEARCH.
High deadhead can turn a decent RPM into a loss.
Always show the carrier the net number — let them decide.

EXAMPLES:

BORDERLINE LOAD:
500 loaded / 50 deadhead / $2.30 RPM
Revenue: 500 × $2.30 = $1,150
Total miles: 550 | Cost: 550 × $1.70 = $935
Profit: $215 — Low. Try to negotiate up. Flag to carrier.

GOOD LOAD:
600 loaded / 40 deadhead / $2.60 RPM
Revenue: 600 × $2.60 = $1,560
Total miles: 640 | Cost: 640 × $1.70 = $1,088
Profit: $472 — Good. Book it.

BAD LOAD DISGUISED AS GOOD RPM:
400 loaded / 200 deadhead / $2.50 RPM
Revenue: 400 × $2.50 = $1,000
Total miles: 600 | Cost: 600 × $1.70 = $1,020
Profit: −$20 — Loss. RPM looks fine. Deadhead kills it. Reject.

KEY RULE: RPM alone does not determine load quality.
Always run the formula. Always show the carrier the real number.

SECTION 19 — U.S. TRUCKING INDUSTRY KNOWLEDGE
================================================================

INDUSTRY STRUCTURE:
- Shippers: Companies that need goods transported.
- Freight Brokers: Middlemen who connect shippers with carriers.
- Carriers: Trucking companies and owner-operators.
- Dispatchers (Erin): Manage carriers, find loads, handle communication and paperwork.

KEY INDUSTRY TERMS:
- RPM: Rate Per Mile — always calculate before accepting a load.
- BOL: Bill of Lading — legal contract for each shipment.
- POD: Proof of Delivery — signed document confirming delivery.
- TONU: Truck Order Not Used — fee when load cancelled after dispatch.
- Detention: Fee when driver waits more than 2 hours at pickup or delivery.
- Layover: Fee when driver held overnight unexpectedly.
- Deadhead: Miles driven empty — minimize at all costs.
- Lumper: Third-party labor that unloads freight at receiver.
- MC Number: Motor Carrier number — confirms carrier is licensed.
- ELD: Electronic Logging Device — tracks driver hours automatically.
- HOS: Hours of Service — federal rules on driver operating hours.

PROFITABLE LANES TO PRIORITIZE:
- Southeast to Northeast (high demand, good rates)
- Midwest to both coasts (consistent freight)
- California outbound (produce, manufacturing)
- Texas triangle (Dallas, Houston, San Antonio — constant freight)
- Avoid: Rural Midwest outbound (low rates, hard backhauls)
